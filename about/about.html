Background Information

Brooklyn Nine-Nine is an American police sitcom/television series that aired on Fox, and later on NBC, from the 17th of September 2013 to the 16th of September 2021. It was created by Dan Goor and Michael Schur and has a total of eight seasons and 153 episodes. The show revolves around the police at a New York City Police Department (NYPD) who are adjusting to life under their new commanding officer, Captain Raymond Holt (Wikipedia).

Significance

The most important thing about this show is that it is a really good form of representation. Four out of seven people in the main cast are people of colour. There are two Black men and two Latina women. The show also has queer reprepresentation with one character being a gay man and another, a bisexual woman. All these characters are well written and relatable, they are a joy to watch, and the stories they tell are sure to make you laugh or warm your heart.
